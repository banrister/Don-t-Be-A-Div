What is 'Don't be a div'?

'Don't be a div' was an idea by two postgraduate students of the University of Sheffield, Chantal and Claire. They were given the opportunity to learn how to code and construct their own website. They hit the drawing board and although were really impressed by the organisation 'CodeFirst: Girls' and the tutorial websites they provided (they have been included in our own best-of compilation lists!), trying to acquire terminology and find similar simpler FAQs at the beginning of the coding process was daunting.

'Don't be a div' aims to be a simple and easy to use comfort blanket for the beginner coder.

  • Are you trying to justify to yourself why you should take the time to learn to code? We'll tell you why you should.
  • Looking to read stories of other beginners' journeys into the code-sphere? You'll find that here.
  • Interested in coding but have absolutely no idea where to start? You'll find that here.
  • You've taken your first steps into learning the building blocks of internet architecture (AKA html and CSS). Are you stuck trying to find tag names, or haven't managed to grasp terminology to help you find answers to your questions on StackOverflow? You can solve your problems here.
  • Spent a long evening with your laptop and coded until you irritate your RSI in your hand and your brain is mush? You can take a break with our game to relieve code rage.
